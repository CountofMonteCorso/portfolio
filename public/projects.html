Projects

What LinkedIn did for Facebook, Portflow.io is doing for Pinterest. Built with React, Portflow.io creates a professional social network designed to serve the needs of painters, artists, coders, and anyone who wants to post their work in a portfolio-style setting. Previously the problem with everyone having individual portfolio sites is that they might be hard to discover, but Portflow.io aims to connect people and make finding quality work easier through one central location. This is my second project with IdentiPop and the project is currently being refined and accepting investors.


My first project working with the IdentiPop. The goal for MacroMeals was to make a site that could store user information on a MySQL database that would return their information to provide optimal meal plans based on macro nutrient needs and fitness goals utilizing a special calculator designed for that purpose. Additionally, it provides recommended recipes that can be favorited and saved on the user's profile or users can search recipes based on ingredients they have in their kitchen (up to 5) thanks to the Spoonacular API. We're still currently polishing MacroMeals, and the project is accepting investors at the present.

The first group project from my education at UCLA was one that spoke to us as poor students: how can we find affordable entertainment near us? Enter Cheap Thrills. Utilizing APIs from Google and Lyft, we grab the location, sort through entertainment by cost-rating on Google, and get you a ride there with Lyft. Also featured on the page is a chat for getting recommendations from other Cheap Thrills users.
